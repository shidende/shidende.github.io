Ph.D. Dissertation

Design and Development of an Accessible Open-Source Augmented Reality Learning Authoring Tool for Applications in Agroecological Settings

Methodology: Integrated Design-Based Research (DBR) with Agile Scrum to iterate on the MirageXR open-source tool. The approach combined usability engineering with systematic accessibility evaluations to ensure inclusive design for non-technical creators.

Impact: Contributed to the CEN Workshop Agreement (CWA 18006) for XR learning standards. The research provides a scalable framework for inclusive AR in agroecology, enabling educators to create accessible learning journeys that bridge the digital divide.
